IV Drip Therapy

Hydration and vitamins delivered intravenously by trained clinicians, with medical screening first and marketing claims left at the door.

IThe treatment

IV drip therapy delivers fluids and vitamins directly into the bloodstream, bypassing digestion entirely. For people who are run down, jet-lagged, training hard or simply feeling depleted, it is the most direct route to rehydration and nutrient top-up available outside a hospital.

Our menu covers the classics done properly: multivitamin cocktails, immunity blends, B-complex and biotin, detox support and skin-brightening formulations. Each drip is administered by a clinician trained in intravenous therapy, in a clinic that also teaches IV skills to healthcare professionals.

We will not tell you a drip cures illness, melts fat on its own or replaces a doctor. What it does, it does honestly: rapid hydration, well-absorbed vitamins and, for many clients, a noticeable lift in how they feel over the following days.

IIWho it suits, honestly

A drip is a supplement by another route, not medicine by another name. Screening comes first, every time.

Consider it if

  • Feeling depleted after travel, illness recovery or a brutal season at work
  • Training loads that outpace your appetite
  • Wanting a vitamin top-up with clinical technique, not a lounge gimmick
  • Skin that reflects a tired system
  • Preferring measured, screened wellness over fads

It is not for

  • Anyone with kidney, heart or certain chronic conditions, unless cleared at screening
  • Pregnancy or breastfeeding, without prior medical advice
  • Anyone seeking treatment for an illness, which needs a doctor, not a drip
  • Needle phobia so strong the hour would be misery, though we are gentle
  • Anyone expecting a drip to replace food, sleep or exercise

IIIYour appointment, step by step

The routine is calm and clinical, and most clients read, work or doze through it.

  1. Screening

    A short health questionnaire and consultation to confirm the drip is appropriate for you, and which formulation actually matches your goal.

  2. Cannulation

    A small cannula placed by a trained clinician, the same technique we teach on our IV therapy and phlebotomy courses. One brief scratch, then comfort.

  3. The infusion

    You settle into a chair while the drip runs. Bring a book, answer emails or close your eyes; the pace is deliberately unhurried.

  4. Afterwards

    The cannula is removed, a small dressing applied, and you carry on with your day. Any aftercare pointers are given in writing.

Safety, before style. Intravenous therapy deserves clinical respect: health screening before every drip, sterile single-use equipment, clinicians trained in IV technique, and formulations from reputable pharmaceutical suppliers. If a drip is not appropriate for you, we will say so and suggest what is.

IVQuestions, answered

Do vitamin drips actually work?

For hydration and vitamin delivery, yes, demonstrably: IV administration bypasses digestion and absorption limits. For grander claims you may have seen elsewhere, we prefer honesty; a drip supports how you feel, it does not perform miracles.

How long does a session take?

Plan for around an hour of calm, though it varies by formulation. Exact timing is confirmed when you book.

Which drip should I choose?

Tell us how you feel and what you are aiming for; the screening consultation exists precisely to match a formulation to you rather than sell you the most expensive bag.

How often can I have one?

It depends on the formulation and on you. Some clients come monthly, some seasonally, some once before a demanding stretch. We will advise a sensible rhythm, and it is never “as often as possible”.

Is it safe?

With screening, sterile technique and trained hands, IV therapy is very well tolerated. The most common effects are a brief cool sensation and, occasionally, a small bruise at the cannula site.
