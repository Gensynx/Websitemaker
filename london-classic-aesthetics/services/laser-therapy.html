Treatments · Skin

Laser Therapy

Focused light, used with medical judgement: for tone, texture, unwanted hair and the stubborn marks skincare cannot reach.

IThe treatment

Laser therapy uses precisely controlled light energy to do what creams fundamentally cannot: reach the structures beneath the surface. Depending on the programme, that means encouraging fresh collagen for smoother, firmer skin, breaking up patches of unwanted pigment, or disabling hair follicles at the root.

Lasers are powerful exactly because they are selective, which makes the operator matter more than the machine. At LCA, laser treatments run inside a doctor-led clinic: your skin is assessed and patch-tested first, settings are chosen for your skin type and goal, and your course is planned rather than improvised.

Most laser work is a course, not a single visit. Skin renews in cycles and hair grows in cycles, so sessions are spaced to work with your biology. We will tell you honestly at consultation what a realistic course looks like for your goal.

IIWho it suits, honestly

Laser rewards patience and punishes shortcuts. It suits people ready to follow a plan, including sun care between sessions.

Consider it if

  • Uneven tone, dullness or early sun damage
  • Texture and fine lines that make-up exaggerates
  • Unwanted facial or body hair you are tired of managing
  • Marks left behind by old breakouts
  • Preferring gradual, cumulative results over drastic ones

It is not for

  • Recently and heavily tanned skin, until it settles
  • Pregnancy, for most programmes
  • Certain medications that increase light sensitivity, screened first
  • Anyone unwilling to use sun protection during a course
  • Expecting one session to do the work of six

IIIYour appointment, step by step

Light is powerful, so the process is deliberately careful. Every laser journey at LCA begins the same way: assessment, then a patch test.

  1. Skin assessment

    We examine your skin type, history and goal, and design the right programme, or advise you honestly if laser is not the best tool for what you want.

  2. Patch test

    A small test area confirms how your skin responds before any full session. It is a non-negotiable step, and your skin will thank you for it.

  3. Your course

    Sessions at planned intervals, each one adjusted to how your skin is responding. Expect warmth and brief pinkness rather than downtime for most programmes.

  4. Protect and maintain

    Daily SPF between sessions is part of the treatment, not an optional extra. Afterwards, we advise on maintenance so results last.

Safety, before style. Every course starts with an assessment and patch test, settings are matched to your skin and reviewed at every session, and treatments take place in a clinic where medical oversight is the default, not an upgrade.

IVQuestions, answered

Does laser treatment hurt?

Most people describe a hot, snapping sensation that stops the moment the laser does. It is brisk rather than painful, and we adjust settings for comfort as well as effect.

How many sessions will I need?

It depends on the goal: hair removal, pigment and rejuvenation each follow different biology. You will get a realistic course estimate at your assessment rather than a number invented to sound appealing.

Is it safe for my skin tone?

That is precisely what the assessment and patch test establish. Settings and suitability differ by skin type, and we will always tell you honestly what is safe for yours.

What will my skin look like straight after?

Commonly a little pink and warm, like a short afternoon in the sun, settling within hours to a day or two depending on the programme.

Can I wear make-up afterwards?

Usually not immediately; we will give you exact timings in your written aftercare, since freshly treated skin deserves a short breather.
