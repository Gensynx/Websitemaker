Dermal Fillers

Volume where it has quietly gone, structure where it was never quite there. Hyaluronic acid fillers, placed by a doctor, in doses your face can carry.

IThe treatment

Dermal fillers are smooth gels of hyaluronic acid, a molecule your skin already makes to hold water. Injected precisely, they restore lost volume, refine a contour or bring balance to a feature that has always bothered you: lips, cheeks, jawline, chin, nose and the hollows beneath the eyes.

The difference between a natural result and an obvious one is rarely the product. It is the plan. At LCA every filler treatment is designed and performed under the care of Dr Hina, working only with MHRA and FDA approved fillers, and dosed to respect your anatomy rather than a trend.

Because hyaluronic acid fillers are gradually broken down by the body, results are long-lasting but not permanent, and most can be dissolved by a clinician if you ever change your mind. That reversibility is part of why we work with them.

IIWho it suits, honestly

Fillers solve specific problems well. They are the right tool far more often when the goal is restoring and balancing, not enlarging.

Consider it if

  • Lips that have thinned, or always lacked definition
  • Flattened cheeks or deepening nasolabial folds
  • A recessed chin or soft jawline you want defined
  • Tear-trough hollows that read as permanent tiredness
  • Asymmetries you have noticed in photographs for years

It is not for

  • Anyone under 18, without exception (UK law)
  • Pregnancy or breastfeeding
  • Active skin infection or inflammation in the area
  • Chasing a dramatically different face, which fillers cannot honestly deliver
  • A same-week special event, since minor swelling needs time to settle

IIIYour appointment, step by step

No filler is placed at LCA without a face-to-face consultation first. Here is the whole journey, so nothing is unfamiliar on the day.

  1. Consultation

    You talk, Dr Hina listens, then examines your facial structure. You get an honest plan with product, placement and expected outcome, and just as importantly, what we would advise against.

  2. Preparation

    Photographs for your clinical record, consent taken properly, and numbing cream applied while you settle in. Most fillers also contain a local anaesthetic.

  3. Treatment

    Precise placement with a fine needle or cannula. You are talked through every stage, and nothing proceeds without your comfort.

  4. Aftercare and review

    You leave with written aftercare and a direct way to reach the clinic. Mild swelling or bruising can appear for a few days and settles on its own; we review results once everything has calmed.

Safety, before style. Fillers are medical treatments, and at LCA they are treated as such: performed in a clinic setting, with MHRA and FDA approved products, emergency protocols in place, and a doctor who will decline a treatment that is not in your interest.

IVQuestions, answered

Will it look obvious?

Not if it is dosed and placed correctly. Our approach is to restore and balance in measured amounts; friends should notice you look well, not ask what you have done. If you want a dramatic change, we will say plainly whether filler can achieve it.

How long do results last?

It varies by product, area and your own metabolism. Hyaluronic acid fillers are gradually absorbed over many months, so results fade softly rather than ending abruptly. You will get a realistic timeframe for your specific plan at consultation.

Does it hurt?

Discomfort is brief and manageable. Numbing cream, fine needles and fillers that contain local anaesthetic keep most clients far more comfortable than they expected.

What if I change my mind?

Most hyaluronic acid fillers can be dissolved by a clinician using hyaluronidase. It is one of the reasons we prefer them, though the aim is a plan you will not want to reverse.

How much does it cost?

Pricing depends on the product and the plan, and is confirmed transparently at consultation before anything is booked. Live prices and availability are also listed on our Fresha page.
