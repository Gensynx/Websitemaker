Academy · CPD Course

Intravenous Therapy Course

Cannulation, infusion protocols and client safety for the booming IV wellness sector, taught by a clinic that runs drips daily.

IAbout this course

IV vitamin therapy has grown from niche to mainstream, and the sector needs practitioners trained to clinical standards rather than weekend-workshop shortcuts. This course teaches intravenous therapy the way it is practised in our own clinic: screened, sterile and unhurried.

The skill at the centre is cannulation: placing intravenous access confidently and kindly. Around it sit the equally important skills of screening clients, selecting formulations honestly and running infusions safely from setup to sign-off.

You train inside a clinic that delivers IV drips as a live service, under the direct supervision of a registered doctor, so protocols are demonstrated where they are actually used.

IIWhat you will cover

Clinical technique first, wellness menu second.

  • Vascular anatomy and site selection: choosing veins well, and what to do when they hide
  • Cannulation technique: insertion, securing and removal, practised until calm
  • Infusion protocols: rates, monitoring and documentation from start to finish
  • Screening and contraindications: who should not receive a drip, and how to say so
  • Formulation literacy: what is actually in vitamin infusions, and honest client conversations
  • Emergencies and complications: recognising and responding, before it is ever needed

IIIHow your training runs

  1. Enquire and check suitability

    Tell us your background and goal. We confirm the right course and entry route for you honestly, including recommending a different starting point if that serves you better.

  2. Theory, properly taught

    Structured study of the science behind the procedure: anatomy, product knowledge, indications, contraindications and complication management, so your hands are guided by understanding.

  3. Supervised practice on live models

    Cannulation and infusion practice on live models under direct doctor supervision, repeated until your technique is smooth and your protocol automatic.

  4. Assessment and certification

    Your competence is assessed before you certify, and you leave with the LCA certificate for your course and a clear path for what to train in next.

IVAccreditation and recognition

CPD-accredited and taught by a registered clinic and training provider that runs IV therapy as a daily service, not a demonstration.

This course pairs directly with Phlebotomy for complete vascular access skills, and with PRP where blood handling meets aesthetics.

VQuestions, answered

Who is this course designed for?

Healthcare professionals extending their scope and practitioners entering the IV wellness sector. Backgrounds and entry routes are confirmed honestly at enquiry.

Will I cannulate real people?

Yes, under direct supervision by a registered doctor, with technique corrected in the moment. Confidence comes from real veins, respectfully practised on.

What is covered about the drips themselves?

Formulation literacy is a full module: what common vitamin infusions contain, what the evidence supports, and how to advise clients without inflating claims.

How does this differ from phlebotomy training?

Phlebotomy is drawing blood out; IV therapy is delivering fluids in, with infusion management around it. The skills are siblings, and many students take both.

How do I enrol?

Enquire through the contact page or email with the course name, and we will confirm suitability and upcoming dates.
