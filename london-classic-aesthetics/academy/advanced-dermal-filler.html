Advanced Dermal Filler

For practitioners ready to move beyond safe basics: cheeks, jawline, chin and non-surgical rhinoplasty, with cannula work and complication management to match.

IAbout this course

Foundation training teaches you to treat safely. The Advanced Dermal Filler course teaches you to treat ambitiously and still safely: full-face assessment, structural areas, cannula technique and the judgement that separates practitioners clients trust with their faces.

The areas covered here, cheeks, jawline, chin, tear troughs and the nose, are where reputations are built and where anatomy demands respect. Accordingly, the ratio of supervision to student stays uncompromisingly high.

As with all LCA practical training, you work on live models in our W1 clinic under the direct supervision of a registered doctor, with your technique challenged and refined case by case.

IIWhat you will cover

The syllabus assumes competence and builds mastery.

  • Full-face assessment: planning treatments that balance features rather than chase single areas
  • Structural augmentation: cheek, chin and jawline techniques for shape that holds
  • Tear trough treatment: patient selection and technique for the least forgiving area in aesthetics
  • Non-surgical rhinoplasty: precision work in a high-stakes zone, taught with appropriate gravity
  • Cannula technique: when and how to trade needle precision for cannula safety
  • Complication management: vascular events, hyaluronidase protocols and emergency readiness

IIIHow your training runs

  1. Enquire and check suitability

    Tell us your background and goal. We confirm the right course and entry route for you honestly, including recommending a different starting point if that serves you better.

  2. Theory, properly taught

    Structured study of the science behind the procedure: anatomy, product knowledge, indications, contraindications and complication management, so your hands are guided by understanding.

  3. Supervised practice on live models

    Advanced techniques performed on live models with the supervising doctor at your shoulder: structural areas, cannula work and full-face planning, signed off case by case.

  4. Assessment and certification

    Your competence is assessed before you certify, and you leave with the LCA certificate for your course and a clear path for what to train in next.

IVAccreditation and recognition

A CPD-accredited advanced course from a registered clinic and training provider, documenting supervised competence in advanced filler techniques.

For practitioners building a formal portfolio, this course sits naturally alongside our regulated routes, including OFQUAL-regulated study and QUALIFI qualifications at Levels 4 to 7.

VQuestions, answered

What do I need before enrolling?

Foundation-level filler training and genuine injecting confidence. If you trained elsewhere, tell us your background at enquiry; we assess suitability honestly and will recommend the foundation course first if the gap is real.

Is non-surgical rhinoplasty really teachable on this course?

Yes, and it is taught with the seriousness it deserves: patient selection, anatomy, technique and complication protocol, practised under direct doctor supervision.

Why does cannula technique matter?

In several advanced areas a cannula meaningfully reduces vascular risk. Knowing when to use which tool is exactly the judgement this course develops.

How are complications covered?

As a full module, not a footnote: recognition, hyaluronidase use, escalation and prevention. Advanced practice means being prepared for the day you hope never comes.

What comes after this course?

Many practitioners add PDO threads or skin boosters to broaden their menu, or progress toward regulated qualifications. We will advise based on where you want your practice to go.
