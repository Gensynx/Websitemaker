Anatomy & Physiology Course

The knowledge underneath every safe treatment: skin, muscle, vasculature and systems, taught for practitioners rather than examiners.

IAbout this course

Every confident answer a good practitioner gives, about safety, about risk, about why a treatment works, rests on anatomy and physiology. This course builds that foundation properly, for people who intend to use it rather than merely pass it.

The teaching is unapologetically applied: facial anatomy is taught the way injectors need it, vasculature the way anyone holding a needle must know it, and skin physiology the way skin practitioners actually use it.

It stands alone as essential grounding, and it slots underneath our practical courses and regulated qualifications as the theory strand everything else assumes.

IIWhat you will cover

The systems that matter to aesthetic practice, taught in practical depth.

  • Skin structure and physiology: layers, function, healing and ageing
  • Facial anatomy for practitioners: muscles, fat compartments and danger zones
  • Vascular and nervous systems: what every injector must map before touching skin
  • Circulatory and lymphatic function: the systems behind IV therapy and recovery
  • Infection control fundamentals: the why behind clinical hygiene, not just the rules
  • Applied revision: anchoring knowledge to the treatments you plan to practise

IIIHow your training runs

  1. Enquire and set your goal

    Tell us where your career is heading; we confirm how this course fits your pathway and what to pair it with.

  2. Structured study

    Clear, sequenced teaching with support materials, built for genuine understanding at a working pace.

  3. Applied learning

    Anatomy anchored to real treatments and demonstrations from clinic practice, so knowledge attaches to use.

  4. Certification and next steps

    Complete the course, receive your CPD certificate, and get a mapped recommendation for the practical training it underpins.

IVAccreditation and recognition

CPD-accredited and delivered by a registered clinic and training academy, with certification recognised as the theory grounding for our practical and regulated routes.

Students frequently pair it with the Dermal Filler Foundation, Phlebotomy or the OFQUAL Level 3 route, and we will happily sequence it for your goal.

VQuestions, answered

Do I need this before practical courses?

It depends on your background: healthcare professionals often have it; career changers usually benefit enormously. We advise honestly at enquiry rather than upsell by default.

Is it heavy on memorisation?

It is taught for understanding and use: anatomy tied to treatments, physiology tied to safety decisions. What you understand, you retain; what you retain, you practise safely with.

Is the course clinical or classroom?

Structured study enriched with clinic-floor demonstration, since we teach inside a working clinic and use that fact deliberately.

Will it help with regulated qualifications?

Directly; anatomy and physiology underpin Level 3 and higher study, and this course is designed to slot underneath those routes.

How do I enrol?

Enquire via the contact page or email with the course name, and we will confirm fit and dates personally.
